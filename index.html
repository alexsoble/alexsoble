Hi.

I'm Alex. I like reading old books, learning new languages, and exploring Chicago. I build web apps and love technology that contributes to the public good.

These are a few projects I've built.

Code for America

I am a 2015 Code for America fellow working with the city of Somerville, Massachusetts on education.


Website:   Code for America.

RailsGirls Chile

With co-organizers from Start-Up Chile, En1Mes, and Groupon, I helped put together the first RailsGirls coding weekend in Chile. Two days, 50 participants, 12 coaches, 6 lightning talks, and a whole lot of beers added up to one excellent weekend of learning and fun.


Websites:   RailsGirlsChile, Storify.

Divvy Braggin'

I'm a huge bikeshare fan, so shortly after Chicago launched its Divvy bikeshare system, I created an app to let members track their mileage. DNAinfoChicago covered the way the app was blocked by Divvy's parent company and then brought back.


Technologies:   Ruby on Rails, Nokogiri web scraping.

Chicago's Local School Councils

Two tweets from the community sparked a new web app for the Chicago Public Schools system, which went on to win "Best Use of Twitter" from Crain's Chicago Business. Read the article in Crain's and the Storify.


Technologies:  Listening carefully, Google Fusion Tables.

#CPSB2S

Our mission: light up the city for back to school. Here's how we did it. Read the Storify.


Technologies:  Picking up the telephone.
